Test Protocol

Directions

We are going to ask you questions and give you a series of tasks that walk you through the deposit process, see how useful,, then ask you to complete some tasks and ask some more questions. Not a graded exam, want to test usability.

Show image of used plastic water bottle
Question: This is your used plastic water bottle, what are you going to do with it?

Show images of mobile app, and reference materials
Question: You see these nearby and remember you have an app on your phone that gives you a bottle deposit for recycling

Question: What are you going to do with the bottle now?
Let user interact with mobile app flow and reference materials

Question: Can you redeem the points for something you want in the Digital Den?
Start over and tell user that the bin is completely full

Question: What are you going to do with the bottle now?
Let user interact with mobile app flow and reference materials

Does the user:
  - Connect device/account to the machine?
  - Deposit bottle?
  - Report a problem?
  - Successfully redeem and purchase?

Feedbacks

Ehren:
Feedback should be “more immediate”, report button on the machine. Seemed to be confused about why there was an interface directly on the bins, some wanted something along the lines of a map, or phone contact for FMS.

Bethany:
Having to scan/tap to get incentive is too much work; Have a separate report button on the machine instead of being a part of the system; Preference of having tiger bucks than just spending at digital den; Some of the process is redundant

Emma:
Recycling is too far, if it looks too complicated trash is the default, Redeem should be on the bottom, confused about the store.

Henry:
Adding a machine number to the machines, as “enter code” doesn’t show much as our lo-fi doesn’t replicate those needs, additionally, they felt the app seemed fine, other than wanting tiger bucks or being able to redeem things other than merch (option to transfer items to either cash or tiger pay). They also didn’t like the machine screen, they’d rather have a flat image of either a map of nearby locations, report button, and a machine number to identify. Additionally, they also added that when the contactless tap doesn’t work, the scan option and enter code option are nice to have but they won’t use it since it is a lot more work.

Reflection

Did the user understand what we are testing? What about their mental model has changed/ What can make this process of changing their model/behaviors more effective?

Questions:

Can you navigate the system on your own?

Is recycling necessary to keep your personal life healthy?

What would make recycling more necessary?

What would make recycling more motivating?

Feedbacks

Ehren:
Testers seemed to navigate fine, Money seemed to be a big motivator, in addition to making it more necessary. Though the popular answer is that recycling is not necessary to one’s personal life.

Bethany:
Most of the testers could get around the app after telling them that it’s an incentive program app. They think that having a physical reward after recycling gives them motivation to recycle in a proper way. Most of the testers find the whole system interesting and they think it will encourage more people to recycle.

Emma:
Money is a main motivator, though there was a few hold-ups in the design. Most notably, we need a “bigger scan button, smaller redeem button” and some confusion about where the home button goes to. Recycling does not seem necessary, regardless of context to personal life.

Henry:
All the testers could get around the app fine even with the first phase of testing. The answer to whether or not it was necessary was mixed. All testers stated that it would be necessary on a community level but not on a personal level. Regarding making it more necessary, most people said to implement a certain punishment for not recycling, similar to nyc’s fine for littering. The same could be implemented for recycling in their opinions. Others said they didn’t know. To make it more motivating, they all said our system would suffice as there was some material reward that benefits them in the short term.
